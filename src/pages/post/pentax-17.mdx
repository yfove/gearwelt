The **Pentax 17** represents a landmark moment in modern photography as the first brand-new film camera from a major manufacturer in decades. Developed under Ricoh Imaging’s "Film Camera Project," this half-frame 35mm camera is designed to appeal to both seasoned analog enthusiasts and a new generation of photographers raised on vertical social media formats. By capturing two **17mm x 24mm** images on a single 35mm frame, the Pentax 17 effectively doubles the capacity of a standard roll—yielding 72 exposures on a 36-exposure film—making it both a creative and economical choice for daily snapshots.

At its heart is the newly developed **HD PENTAX HF 25mm f/3.5 lens**, which provides a 37mm equivalent field of view in full-frame terms. This lens draws inspiration from the legendary optics of the Pentax Espio Mini and the Ricoh Auto Half, but incorporates modern HD coatings to ensure high contrast and sharpness. To maintain the tactile experience of classic film bodies, the Pentax 17 features a manual film advance lever with a satisfying mechanical action, alongside physical dials for ISO selection and exposure compensation.

The camera utilizes a **six-zone focusing system** that simplifies the shooting process while offering creative control. Users can quickly toggle between presets—ranging from a dedicated **0.25m Macro mode** to a "Mountain" setting for infinity focus—by selecting the corresponding icons on the lens barrel. For composition, the bright Albada-type optical viewfinder includes parallax compensation frame lines, ensuring accuracy even when shooting at the camera's minimum focusing distance.
