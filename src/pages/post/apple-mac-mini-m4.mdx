The **Apple Mac Mini (M4)** represents the first major design overhaul for Apple’s smallest desktop in over a decade. Now measuring a mere **5 by 5 inches**, the new enclosure is less than half the size of its predecessor, yet it packs significantly more performance thanks to the latest M4 and M4 Pro silicon. It is a masterclass in thermal engineering and efficiency, proving that a desktop doesn't need to be large to be powerful.

The shift to the **M4 chip** brings desktop-class speed to everyday tasks and creative workflows alike. With an improved CPU and a GPU that supports hardware-accelerated ray tracing, the Mac Mini can handle everything from 4K video editing to high-end gaming with ease. For the first time, the base model also starts with **16GB of unified memory**, ensuring smoother multitasking right out of the box.

Connectivity has also been modernized. The Mac Mini now features **front-facing ports**—including two USB-C ports and a high-impedance headphone jack—for easier access. On the rear, the M4 Pro model supports **Thunderbolt 5**, offering data transfer speeds up to 120 Gbps. As Apple’s first carbon-neutral Mac, the new Mini isn't just a win for desk space and performance; it’s a benchmark for sustainable high-performance computing in 2024.
